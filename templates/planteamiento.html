Business Objectives

To develop a machine learning model capable of predicting potential failures in the public lighting system by analyzing citizen reports and energy consumption data, with the purpose of optimizing maintenance operations and reducing response times.

Key Questions

  • Which areas have the highest probability of failures?
  • Is there a relationship between energy consumption and reported failures?
  • Which type of light fixture fails most frequently?
  • Can patterns be identified based on location or the technology used?
  • How can preventive maintenance be prioritized efficiently?

Data Evaluation

The dataset contains relevant information regarding:

  • Number of light fixtures,
  • Type of technology (LED, sodium, halogen),
  • Distribution by location,
  • Percentage of LED light fixtures.

The data is useful for identifying consumption patterns and potential critical areas. However, it would be ideal to complement it with:

  • Citizen reports,
  • Maintenance history,
  • Detailed energy consumption data,
  • Dates and frequency of failures.

Feasibility Analysis

The project is technically feasible because the available data enables predictive analysis through machine learning techniques. However, some limitations must be considered:

  • Possible lack of historical failure data,
  • Incomplete or outdated information,
  • The need to integrate multiple information sources.

Despite these limitations, the project can contribute significantly to improving public lighting management, reducing operational costs, and enabling more efficient preventive maintenance strategies.
