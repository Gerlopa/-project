Data Engineering

The data engineering phase focuses on properly preparing the information for the development of the machine learning model. According to the CRISP-ML methodology, this stage includes data selection, cleaning, transformation, and documentation processes in order to maximize data quality and predictive value.

Data Selection

The project uses the monthly consolidated public lighting datasets of Bogotá corresponding to the year 2023. The data includes information related to:

  • Total number of light fixtures.
  • Technology type used (LED, sodium, metal halide).
  • Distribution by locality.
  • Percentage of LED light fixtures.

These variables were selected because of their relevance for identifying technological modernization patterns and potential critical areas within the urban electrical infrastructure.

Data Cleaning

During this stage, empty or unnecessary columns were removed, inconsistencies in variable names were corrected, and incomplete records were managed. In addition, the integrity of numerical data types was verified to avoid errors during analytical processing.

The process also included the removal of empty rows and the standardization of formats among the different monthly datasets to ensure consistency across the consolidated historical records.

Data Transformation

The monthly datasets were integrated into a single consolidated dataset that enables temporal analysis of the public lighting system behavior.

Additionally, new derived variables were generated through feature engineering techniques, including:

  • Ratio of old technologies compared to the total number of light fixtures.
  • Percentage of LED modernization by locality.
  • Potential failure risk indicators.
  • Monthly infrastructure variation indicators.

These transformations facilitate pattern identification and improve the predictive potential of machine learning models.

Documentation

All transformations applied to the data were documented to ensure project reproducibility and facilitate future model updates. This practice helps maintain traceability of decisions made during data processing and reduces risks associated with analytical inconsistencies.
